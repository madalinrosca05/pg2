Keep Walking

The road wasn’t supposed to be this long.

At least, that’s what Elio told himself as his shoes scraped against the cracked pavement. Each step felt heavier than the last, like something unseen clung to his ankles, begging him to stop.

He kept his eyes forward.

Not because there was anything worth seeing—but because looking down meant thinking. And thinking meant remembering.

The promises.
The mistakes.
The version of himself he used to believe in.

A voice lingered in his mind, not quite his own.

Keep walking.

He laughed softly, bitterly.
“Why?” he muttered. “What’s even ahead?”

The wind answered instead—dry, hollow, like breath forced through tired lungs.

Elio paused.

For a moment, just a moment, he considered sitting down. Letting the road end where he stood. Letting the weight win.

His knees bent slightly—

—and then he straightened again.

Keep walking.

The words weren’t kind. They weren’t encouraging. They didn’t promise things would get better.

They just… insisted.

So he walked.

Past the places that reminded him of who he failed to be.
Past the silence where laughter used to live.
Past the invisible echoes of expectations he never asked for.

Each step hurt.

Not sharply—but constantly. Like a dull ache that had become part of him.

“Does it ever stop?” he asked aloud.

No answer came this time.

Only the rhythm of his footsteps.

Step.
Step.
Step.

Eventually, the road began to change. Not dramatically—just enough that he noticed. The cracks grew smaller. The air felt… lighter.

Or maybe he was just getting used to the weight.

Elio slowed, confused.

He wasn’t happy.
He wasn’t healed.
He wasn’t even sure he was better.

But he was still moving.

And somehow, that mattered.

He took a breath—deep, shaky—and felt something loosen in his chest. Not gone… just less tight.

“…okay,” he whispered.

Not I’m okay.
Just… okay.

And with that, he kept walking.
